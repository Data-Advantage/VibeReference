# Conversation Intelligence & Meeting Recording Platforms: Gong, Chorus, Avoma, Fathom, Otter, Fireflies, Granola, Read, Clari Copilot

[⬅️ Marketing & SEO Overview](../marketing-and-seo/)

If you're running a B2B sales motion in 2026, you're recording meetings — sales discovery calls, demos, customer success QBRs, internal sync. The recordings drive coaching, deal forecasting, customer-feedback synthesis, post-call note generation, and CRM auto-fill. The category split that matters in 2026: **enterprise revenue intelligence** (Gong, Chorus, Clari Copilot — full sales coaching + deal-risk + analytics), **mid-market conversation intelligence** (Avoma — sales + CS focus at lower price), **AI meeting note-takers** (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom — broader meeting capture, not sales-specific), and **modern AI-first** (Granola, Read, Krisp Notes — designed for the AI-meetings-everywhere era).

The category re-shaped fast in 2024-2026. Gong is still leader for enterprise sales; mid-market saw Fathom and Granola explode (free or near-free; AI-first UX); generic note-takers (Otter, Fireflies) compete on price + breadth. Pick by team type + budget + the specific outcome (coaching vs. forecasting vs. note-taking vs. CRM auto-fill).

The first decision is **what outcome you actually want**:
- "Sales coaching at scale + revenue intelligence" → Gong / Chorus / Clari Copilot
- "Mid-market sales + CS conversation intelligence under $200/seat/mo" → Avoma
- "Free or near-free meeting notes for general productivity" → Fathom / Otter / Fireflies / Granola
- "Bundled with our Zoom / Teams / Google" → use the bundled AI companion first

## Decide What You Need First

Conversation tools are not interchangeable. The same SaaS at different stages and team types has very different correct picks.

### Solo founder / pre-Series A (the 30% case)

You take 5-15 sales calls / week. You want notes auto-generated, action items captured, transcript searchable. Coaching analytics + revenue forecasting are overkill.

**Pick: Fathom (free) or Granola (free Mac).** Both produce excellent AI summaries; Fathom integrates with Salesforce / HubSpot; Granola has the cleanest UX for Mac users. You'll outgrow these around 5+ reps where coaching matters.

### Mid-market sales team ($5M-50M ARR) (the 30% case)

You have 5-25 reps. You want call recording + AI summaries + Salesforce integration + light coaching. You don't have $50K/yr budget for Gong.

**Pick: Avoma.** $19-149/seat depending on tier. Good Salesforce + HubSpot integration. AI summaries + coaching + scorecards. 90% of Gong's value at 20% of the price for this stage.

### Enterprise sales team ($50M+ ARR) (the 25% case)

You have 50+ reps. Sales coaching is a function. Revenue forecasting + deal-risk analysis matter. CMO + CRO want analytics dashboards on conversation patterns.

**Pick: Gong.** It's expensive ($1.5K-2.5K/seat/yr) but the depth of sales coaching, deal-risk insights, and revenue intelligence is unmatched. Chorus (ZoomInfo) and Clari Copilot are alternatives if you're already in those ecosystems.

### General productivity (cross-team note-taking) (the 15% case)

Not just sales — all-hands meetings, team syncs, customer interviews. You want AI notes everywhere.

**Pick: Bundled AI** (Zoom AI Companion / M365 Copilot / Google Meet Gemini) **+ Fathom / Otter for non-bundled use cases.** The bundled options are getting good in 2026; specialized tools fill gaps.

## Provider Deep-Dives

### Gong

The category leader. Founded 2015. Used by Outreach, Workday, ADP, etc. Sales coaching + revenue intelligence at enterprise scale.

Strengths:

- **Best-in-class sales coaching** — call summaries, talk-listen ratios, key moments, competitor mentions, objection handling
- **Revenue intelligence layer**: deal risk, pipeline insights, forecast accuracy
- **Trackers**: monitor specific keywords / topics across thousands of calls (e.g., "every time a prospect says 'integration', surface those clips")
- Strong CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics)
- Dedicated AI for sales-specific use cases
- Strong analytics + dashboards
- High-fidelity transcription
- Industry-leading customer base (signal that it works at scale)

Weaknesses:

- **Expensive** — $1.5K-2.5K/seat/yr. For a 50-rep team, that's $75K-125K/yr.
- Sales-led contracting; 12-month minimum commits
- Heavyweight implementation
- Overkill for SMB / small sales teams
- Some customers complain about UI bloat as features accreted

Use Gong when:

- You're $50M+ ARR; serious sales org
- Sales coaching is a function (you have a Sales Enablement team)
- You can budget $100K+/yr for the program
- ROI justified by deal velocity / win rate / forecast accuracy improvements

Avoid when:

- Pre-Series-B; under-staffed sales coaching
- Budget can't justify enterprise pricing

### Chorus (ZoomInfo)

Acquired by ZoomInfo in 2021. Bundled with their broader sales intelligence platform. Strong for ZoomInfo customers.

Strengths:

- **Tight integration with ZoomInfo data** (prospect intelligence + conversation intelligence in one platform)
- Solid conversation intelligence + coaching
- Good for sales orgs already standardized on ZoomInfo

Weaknesses:

- Less dominant than Gong; some say less polished
- ZoomInfo bundling helps if you're a customer; doesn't help if you're not
- Pricing similar to Gong (enterprise-tier)

Use Chorus when:

- You're a ZoomInfo customer
- Bundled pricing makes it cheaper than Gong

### Clari Copilot (formerly Wingman)

Clari acquired Wingman in 2023; rebranded as Clari Copilot. Bundled with Clari's revenue platform.

Strengths:

- **Tight integration with Clari** revenue forecasting
- Conversation intelligence + deal-risk in same platform
- Strong for revenue ops teams already using Clari

Weaknesses:

- Less mature standalone CI than Gong
- Best when bundled with Clari; less compelling alone

Use Clari Copilot when:

- You're a Clari customer
- Bundled story unlocks unified revenue intelligence

### Avoma

Founded 2017. Mid-market sweet spot. Conversation intelligence + meeting collaboration at SMB-friendly pricing.

Strengths:

- **Pricing accessible to SMB** ($19-149/seat) — vs Gong's $1.5K+
- Conversation intelligence (transcription, summaries, key moments, coaching scorecards)
- Customer success use case (not just sales)
- Salesforce + HubSpot integrations
- AI-driven note-taking + topic detection
- Speaker-attribution accurate
- Solid for small-to-mid sales teams

Weaknesses:

- Less depth than Gong on revenue intelligence
- Smaller ecosystem
- Coaching scorecards less battle-tested than Gong's

Use Avoma when:

- Mid-market SaaS ($5M-50M ARR)
- You want conversation intelligence + AI notes without Gong pricing
- Sales + CS coverage matters

### Modjo

EU-origin (France). Mid-market conversation intelligence with strong EU footprint.

Use Modjo when:

- EU-based sales team
- French / European data residency matters

### Fathom

Free AI meeting note-taker. Founded 2020. Hit hard in indie / SMB market starting 2023.

Strengths:

- **Free forever** — including unlimited recording, transcription, AI summary
- Supports Zoom / Google Meet / Microsoft Teams
- AI summaries + action items + topic detection
- Salesforce / HubSpot / Slack integrations (some on Pro tier)
- Search across all your past calls
- Highlights + clip-creation
- Pro tier ($19/seat/mo) for team features

Weaknesses:

- Less depth on coaching analytics than Gong / Avoma
- "Sales-aware" features are basic vs dedicated CI tools
- Free-tier business model — long-term sustainability uncertain (though VC-funded)

Use Fathom when:

- Solo founder / small team taking sales calls
- You want free or near-free
- General-purpose AI notes across all meeting types

### Otter.ai

Mainstream AI transcription. Founded 2016.

Strengths:

- Mature; battle-tested
- Multiple meeting platforms
- Browser extension + mobile apps
- Live transcription during meetings
- Decent free tier

Weaknesses:

- Less differentiated as Fathom and others have caught up
- AI summary quality has lagged Fathom in 2025-2026 reviews
- Mainstream brand; less indie love

Use Otter when:

- Already in your stack
- You want established mainstream

### Fireflies.ai

Affordable mainstream meeting AI. Founded 2016.

Strengths:

- **Cheap** ($10+/mo)
- Multi-platform meeting capture
- AI summaries
- Apps for various integrations

Weaknesses:

- Less polished than Fathom / Granola
- AI quality decent but not best-in-class

Use Fireflies when:

- Cost-conscious team
- Mainstream alternatives unaffordable

### Granola

Mac-native AI notes. Founded 2023. Hit indie + product audiences hard in 2024-2026 with clean UX.

Strengths:

- **Cleanest UX** in the category
- Mac-native (better local performance, privacy)
- Doesn't auto-join meetings — you take notes yourself; Granola enriches with AI from audio
- Great for "I want my notes + AI assist, not bots in meetings"
- Free tier; $18/mo Pro
- Privacy-focused (local-first model option)

Weaknesses:

- Mac-only currently
- Less integration with Salesforce / HubSpot for sales workflows
- Designed for note-taker-driven flow (not auto-record-everything)

Use Granola when:

- Mac user
- You prefer to drive your notes; AI augments
- Privacy / less-bot-in-meetings preference

### Read.ai

Cross-channel AI summaries (meetings + emails + chats). Founded 2018.

Strengths:

- **Cross-channel summaries** (not just meetings)
- Sentiment analysis + meeting effectiveness scoring
- Pricing reasonable

Weaknesses:

- Sentiment / engagement scoring — useful or noise depending on context
- Smaller than Otter / Fireflies

Use Read when:

- You want meeting + email + chat summaries unified
- Sentiment / engagement metrics interesting to you

### Sembly

Action-item-heavy AI notes. Founded 2019.

Strengths:

- Strong action-item extraction
- Task management integration
- Mid-tier pricing

Use Sembly when:

- Meetings produce action items that need tracking

### Spinach

Sprint / standup specialized AI notes. Founded 2021.

Strengths:

- **Engineering team focus** — sprint planning, standup, retro
- Jira integration
- Action-item extraction tied to engineering tickets

Use Spinach when:

- Engineering team running daily standups; want async summaries

### Zoom AI Companion / Microsoft Teams Copilot / Google Meet Gemini

Bundled AI in your existing video platform.

Strengths:

- **Free with your existing license** (or modest add-on)
- Native to the meeting platform
- Acceptable summaries + transcription
- No additional vendor onboarding

Weaknesses:

- Less specialized than dedicated tools
- Cross-platform users need multiple bundled tools (or one dedicated)
- Sales coaching depth nonexistent

Use bundled AI when:

- You're standardized on one platform
- You don't need sales-specific analytics
- You want zero additional vendor footprint

## What These Tools Won't Do

**Don't expect AI summaries to replace human note-taking for high-stakes meetings.** Board meetings, customer escalations, deal negotiations — review the AI output; don't rely solely.

**Don't expect AI to coach reps automatically.** Tools surface insights; humans do coaching. Saying "Gong told me you talked too much" without a coach is an empty gesture.

**Don't expect transcription to be perfect.** Even the best tools (Otter, Fathom) get 5-15% errors on technical jargon, accents, and overlapping speech. Edit before relying.

**Don't expect every meeting to be recorded.** Some meetings (executive 1:1s, sensitive customer convos) shouldn't be recorded. Set policies; default-off where appropriate.

**Don't expect AI notes to replace CRM hygiene.** Auto-fill helps but reps still need to qualify, categorize, and update opportunity stages thoughtfully.

**Don't expect tools to handle compliance for you.** Recording laws vary (one-party-consent vs all-party-consent). EU GDPR + state laws (Illinois, Florida, Oregon, Pennsylvania, etc.) require participant consent. Tools surface a notification; the legal exposure remains yours.

**Don't expect bots in meetings to be welcomed by everyone.** Some prospects refuse to be recorded. Have a graceful fallback (manual notes) for those situations.

## Pragmatic Stack Patterns

### Indie / Solo Founder

- Fathom (free) for everything
- Granola if Mac + privacy preference
- Total: $0/mo

### SMB Sales Team (5-15 reps, <$5M ARR)

- Fathom Pro ($19/seat/mo) or Avoma starter ($19/seat/mo)
- Salesforce / HubSpot integration
- Total: $200-500/mo

### Mid-Market ($5-50M ARR, 15-50 reps)

- Avoma at higher tier ($60-150/seat/mo) for coaching scorecards
- OR Gong at lower tier if budget allows
- Total: $1K-7K/mo

### Enterprise ($50M+ ARR, 50+ reps)

- Gong (or Chorus / Clari Copilot if ecosystem-aligned)
- Bundled platform AI for non-sales meetings
- Total: $75K-300K+/yr

### Cross-Team Productivity (Engineering, Product, CS, Sales all want notes)

- Bundled AI (Zoom Companion + M365 Copilot)
- Fathom / Otter for non-bundled meetings
- Spinach for engineering standups
- Granola for Mac-leaning teams
- Total: $0-200/mo per person depending on tools

## Decision Framework: Five Questions

1. **Sales-specific or general productivity?**
   - Sales-specific: Gong / Chorus / Avoma / Clari Copilot
   - General: Fathom / Otter / Fireflies / Granola
   - Both: bundled platform + Avoma for sales

2. **What's your scale?**
   - Solo / <10 reps: Fathom or Granola (free)
   - 10-50 reps: Avoma
   - 50+ reps: Gong or alternative enterprise

3. **What's your existing CRM / sales engagement stack?**
   - Salesforce + Outreach / Salesloft: Gong integrates best
   - HubSpot: Avoma + Fathom integrate cleanly
   - ZoomInfo: Chorus is your bundled option
   - Clari: Clari Copilot

4. **Mac team or cross-platform?**
   - Mac-leaning + privacy: Granola
   - Cross-platform: anything else

5. **Free is critical?**
   - Yes: Fathom (free)
   - No: any tier

## Verdict

**Solo / pre-revenue**: Fathom (free) is the default. Granola if you're Mac.

**SMB sales team ($1-5M ARR)**: Fathom Pro or Avoma starter. Pricing matters; coaching scorecards aren't critical yet.

**Mid-market ($5-50M ARR)**: Avoma is the sweet spot. Gong if budget allows + you have a sales enablement function.

**Enterprise ($50M+)**: Gong. The category leader for a reason. Chorus for ZoomInfo customers; Clari Copilot for Clari customers.

**Cross-team productivity**: bundled AI in Zoom / Teams / Google Meet + Fathom for the gaps.

The most common mistakes are:

1. **Buying Gong before you have a sales coaching function.** Gong's value is partly the analytics, but mostly the sales-coach-driven workflow built around it. Without a coach, it's an expensive transcript library.
2. **Recording without consent.** Compliance traps in EU (GDPR), California (CIPA), Illinois (BIPA), Florida (two-party consent). Tools notify; the legal exposure remains yours. Brief reps.
3. **Bots-in-every-meeting culture.** Some meetings (sensitive customer escalations, executive 1:1s) shouldn't be recorded. Set policy; default-off in some surfaces.
4. **Treating AI summaries as ground truth.** They're 80-95% accurate; the 5-20% gap matters in high-stakes situations.
