# Affiliate Marketing Tools: Rewardful, PartnerStack, Tolt, Tapfiliate, Impact, FirstPromoter, Refersion, ReferralCandy

[⬅️ Marketing & SEO Overview](../marketing-and-seo/)

If you're building a SaaS in 2026 and trying to pick an affiliate marketing tool, this is the consolidated comparison. Affiliate programs are the line item founders skip until a creator emails asking "do you have a referral program?" and you scramble to build one in Stripe Coupons + Notion. Most indie SaaS over-engineer with PartnerStack (overkill at the indie tier) when Rewardful or Tolt at $89/mo would have served them through $5M ARR. Pick the right shape and affiliate revenue compounds quietly; pick wrong and you spend more on the tool than the channel earns.

The first decision is **what shape of affiliate program you're running**. SaaS with recurring revenue (Rewardful / Tolt / FirstPromoter), e-commerce with one-time purchases (Refersion / ReferralCandy), partner ecosystems with B2B agencies (PartnerStack / Impact), and consumer-affiliate networks (ShareASale / CJ) are four different problems with overlapping tools. Most indie SaaS in 2026 need the first.

## Decide What You Need First

Affiliate tools are not interchangeable. Pick by business model and payment processor.

### SaaS recurring affiliate (the 70% case for indie SaaS)
You sell SaaS subscriptions on Stripe / Paddle. You want to pay affiliates a % of recurring revenue (e.g., 20% lifetime, or 30% for first 12 months).

Right tools:
- **Rewardful** — modern indie default; Stripe-native
- **Tolt** — cheaper alternative
- **FirstPromoter** — supports Paddle / Chargebee
- **Tapfiliate** — general-purpose

### E-commerce one-time affiliate (the 15% case)
You sell physical or digital one-time products. You want to pay affiliates a % of the sale.

Right tools:
- **Refersion** — Shopify-friendly
- **ReferralCandy** — customer referrals
- **Tapfiliate** — works for both

### Partner ecosystem (the 10% case)
You have B2B partners (agencies, consultants, integrators) who refer or co-sell. You need tiered programs, MDF (market development funds), partner portals.

Right tools:
- **PartnerStack** — mid-market default
- **Impact (Impact.com)** — enterprise
- **Crossbeam** — partnership data layer (different but adjacent)

### Customer-referral program (the 5% case)
Your existing customers refer friends. You want to give both sides a credit.

Right tools:
- **ReferralCandy** — customer-referral specialist
- **Friendbuy** — customer-referral
- Built-in: Rewardful and Tolt support customer-referral as a sub-program
- DIY: per-customer referral codes in your app

For most indie SaaS in 2026: **Rewardful or Tolt for SaaS-affiliate; PartnerStack only when you''ve graduated to formal partner programs**. Skip Impact until enterprise scale.

## Provider Deep-Dives

### Rewardful — Modern Indie SaaS Default
Rewardful has become the indie SaaS default in 2026. Native Stripe integration; clean UI; founder-friendly pricing.

Strengths:
- Stripe-native (auto-tracks customers + subscriptions)
- Recurring affiliate commissions (lifetime / fixed period / first payment)
- $89/mo Starter (10K MTU); $149/mo Growth
- Clean dashboard
- Affiliate sign-up portal customizable
- Cookie-based + UTM tracking
- Self-service affiliate management

Weaknesses:
- Stripe-only (doesn''t work outside Stripe)
- No partner-portal features (vs PartnerStack)
- Less suited for e-commerce / one-time

Pick when: you''re indie SaaS / SMB on Stripe, want recurring affiliate program, single-product focus.

### Tolt — Cheaper Indie Alternative
Tolt emerged as a Rewardful alternative with similar shape and lower entry price.

Strengths:
- Free tier (5 affiliates)
- $59/mo paid tier
- Stripe / Paddle / Lemon Squeezy support
- Modern UI
- Active development
- AI-generated affiliate content

Weaknesses:
- Smaller community than Rewardful
- Some integrations less mature

Pick when: similar use case as Rewardful; pricing matters; OK with newer product.

### Tapfiliate — General-Purpose
Tapfiliate is more general-purpose. Works for SaaS + e-commerce + service businesses.

Strengths:
- Flexible (subscription / one-time / hybrid)
- $89/mo Essential
- 30+ integrations (Stripe, Recurly, Chargebee, WooCommerce, Shopify)
- Multi-currency
- Good API

Weaknesses:
- Less SaaS-specific than Rewardful
- UI feels older

Pick when: hybrid product (SaaS + e-commerce) or need flexibility beyond Stripe-only.

### FirstPromoter — SaaS Without Stripe
FirstPromoter is similar to Rewardful but supports Paddle / Chargebee / Recurly natively.

Strengths:
- Multi-billing-platform (Paddle / Chargebee / Recurly / Stripe)
- $59/mo Starter
- Recurring commissions
- Good API
- Modern UI

Weaknesses:
- Smaller community than Rewardful
- Stripe integration less polished than Rewardful''s

Pick when: you''re on Paddle (per [tax-compliance-tools](../auth-and-payments/tax-compliance-tools.md)) or Chargebee; need affiliate program for those.

### PartnerStack — Mid-Market Partner Ecosystems
PartnerStack is partner-relationship-management (PRM) for B2B. Tiered programs, partner portals, MDF, training.

Strengths:
- Real partner-portal capability
- Tiered commission structures
- Training / certification tracking
- MDF tracking
- Partner-recruitment marketplace
- Strong reporting

Weaknesses:
- Custom pricing (typically $1K-5K/mo)
- Sales-led implementation
- Overkill for indie SaaS
- Heavy product surface

Pick when: you''re mid-market with formal partner program (10+ active partners; tiered structures; MDF).

### Impact (Impact.com) — Enterprise
Impact is enterprise affiliate / partnership platform. Used by large e-commerce + SaaS.

Strengths:
- Enterprise scale
- Multi-channel partnerships
- Influencer marketing module
- Compliance / fraud detection

Weaknesses:
- Custom pricing ($$$$)
- Sales-led
- Not for indie

Pick when: enterprise; budget supports.

### Refersion — Shopify-Native
Refersion is built for e-commerce affiliate programs, especially Shopify.

Strengths:
- Native Shopify integration
- E-commerce focused
- $99/mo Starter
- Affiliate marketplace

Weaknesses:
- Less suited for SaaS
- Shopify-centric

Pick when: Shopify e-commerce; not for SaaS.

### ReferralCandy — Customer Referrals
ReferralCandy specializes in customer-referral programs (existing customer → friend).

Strengths:
- Customer-referral focused
- Native Shopify / WooCommerce
- $59/mo
- Reward both sides automatically

Weaknesses:
- Customer-referral specific
- Less suited for affiliate marketers

Pick when: e-commerce; customer-referral specifically.

### Reditus — Indie SaaS Affiliate Marketplace
Reditus is newer; combines affiliate platform + a marketplace where indie SaaS list their programs.

Strengths:
- Free tier (1 program)
- $69/mo paid
- Marketplace = discoverability
- Indie SaaS focused

Weaknesses:
- Newer / smaller
- Less feature breadth

Pick when: you want both affiliate platform AND marketplace listing for inbound affiliates.

### LinkMink — Lightweight Stripe-Native
LinkMink is a lighter Stripe-native affiliate tool.

Strengths:
- Simple
- Stripe-native
- $0-149/mo

Weaknesses:
- Limited features
- Smaller community

Pick when: very early; want minimal solution.

### DIY (Stripe Coupons + spreadsheet)
For pre-launch / earliest stage, you can DIY:

- Create unique Stripe coupon per affiliate
- Track redemptions manually
- Pay out manually via Stripe Connect or PayPal

Pros: free; flexible
Cons: doesn''t scale beyond ~10 affiliates; manual reconciliation

Pick when: <10 affiliates; very early; testing the channel.

## What Affiliate Tools Won''t Do

- **Replace your affiliate-program design.** Tools track + pay; you decide commission structure, terms, audience. Per [LaunchWeek affiliate-program](https://www.launchweek.com/3-distribute/affiliate-program).
- **Replace creator outreach.** Affiliates don''t materialize; you recruit them.
- **Be free of fraud risk.** Self-referral, cookie stuffing, fake accounts are real. Most tools have basic fraud detection; not all of it.
- **Replace tax compliance.** Affiliate payments are 1099-able in US; per [tax-compliance-tools](../auth-and-payments/tax-compliance-tools.md). Tools provide reports; you file.
- **Replace agreement terms.** Affiliate agreement (legal terms, brand guidelines, prohibited promotion methods) is your work.

## Pragmatic Stack Patterns

**Indie SaaS, basic affiliate program**:
- Rewardful or Tolt
- Stripe payouts via Stripe Connect
- Total: $59-149/mo

**SaaS not on Stripe**:
- FirstPromoter or Tapfiliate
- Paddle / Chargebee integration
- Total: $59-89/mo

**Customer-referral program**:
- Built into Rewardful / Tolt as sub-program
- Or ReferralCandy if e-commerce
- Total: bundled or $59/mo

**Mid-market with partners**:
- PartnerStack
- Plus affiliate-tier still on Rewardful (separate program)
- Total: $1K+/mo

**E-commerce**:
- Refersion or Tapfiliate
- Total: $89-99/mo

**Marketplace listing for discoverability**:
- Reditus + your main affiliate tool
- Total: $69 + main tool

## Decision Framework: Three Questions

1. **What''s your billing platform?** → Stripe: Rewardful / Tolt. Paddle / Chargebee: FirstPromoter. Shopify: Refersion. Multi-platform: Tapfiliate.
2. **What kind of partners?** → Affiliates / creators: Rewardful / Tolt. Existing customers: same with sub-program. B2B partner ecosystem: PartnerStack.
3. **What''s your scale?** → Indie: Rewardful / Tolt. Mid-market with partners: PartnerStack. Enterprise: Impact.

Three questions, three picks. The 90% answer for indie SaaS in 2026 is **Rewardful or Tolt on Stripe; FirstPromoter on Paddle / Chargebee; PartnerStack only when partner program graduates from "affiliate links" to "structured partnership"**. Skip Impact until enterprise.

## Verdict

For most readers building a SaaS in 2026:
- **Default for indie SaaS / SMB on Stripe**: Rewardful or Tolt.
- **SaaS not on Stripe**: FirstPromoter.
- **Multi-platform / hybrid**: Tapfiliate.
- **Mid-market partner ecosystem**: PartnerStack.
- **Enterprise**: Impact.com.
- **E-commerce**: Refersion or ReferralCandy.
- **Customer referral specifically**: Rewardful sub-program or ReferralCandy.
- **Pre-launch / very early**: DIY with Stripe coupons.

The hidden cost in affiliate tools isn''t the seat fee — it''s **affiliate fraud + low-quality affiliates.** Cookie stuffing, self-referral, fake-account abuse, and creators who buy traffic and refund all eat into the channel''s real ROI. The discipline of affiliate vetting + fraud detection + clear terms (refund clawback after Y days; minimum-cookie window; prohibited tactics list) determines whether the program nets value or noise. Tooling helps; policy matters more.
