# Webinar & Virtual Event Platforms: Zoom Webinars, Demio, Livestorm, ON24, Goldcast, Bizzabo, Hopin

[⬅️ Marketing & SEO Overview](../marketing-and-seo/)

If you're running a B2B SaaS in 2026, you'll likely run webinars or virtual events — product demos, lead-gen webinars, customer onboarding, partner training, virtual conferences. The naive approach: use Zoom Meeting, paste recording link in email. The structured approach: use a webinar platform with registration pages, automated reminders, on-demand replay, polls/Q&A engagement, CRM integration, and analytics. The right pick depends on whether you want simple webinars (Zoom Webinars / Demio / Livestorm), large virtual events (Hopin / Goldcast / Bizzabo), or webinars-as-revenue-content (ON24 enterprise).

## Decide What You Need First

### Lead-gen webinars — registration → live → replay (the 50% case)
You're running webinars to generate / nurture leads. Need registration page, reminders, attendance tracking, replay.

Right tools:
- **Zoom Webinars** — mainstream default
- **Demio** — modern UX
- **Livestorm** — browser-based
- **GoToWebinar** — legacy

### Virtual conference / multi-session event (the 15% case)
You're running a 1-3 day event with multiple sessions, networking, sponsors.

Right tools:
- **Hopin** — virtual conference leader
- **Goldcast** — modern B2B virtual events
- **Bizzabo** — hybrid event leader
- **BigMarker** — virtual summit

### Evergreen / automated webinars (the 10% case)
You want webinars that play on demand 24/7 with simulated live experience.

Right tools:
- **EverWebinar** (WebinarJam) — leader
- **WebinarNinja** — alternative
- **Demio** — has automated replay mode

### Enterprise demand-gen (the 10% case)
You're running 50+ webinars/year with deep CRM integration, content hub, ABM signals.

Right tools:
- **ON24** — enterprise leader
- **GoldCast** — modern alternative

### Internal / training webinars (the 10% case)
You're using webinars for partner training, customer onboarding, internal events.

Right tools:
- **Microsoft Teams Webinars** (M365 native)
- **Zoom Webinars** (default)
- **Demio** (training-friendly)

### Streaming + multi-platform (the 5% case)
You want to stream simultaneously to YouTube + LinkedIn + Twitter + your site.

Right tools:
- **StreamYard** — leader
- **Restream** — alternative
- **Vimeo Events** — for premium production

## Provider Deep-Dives

### Zoom Webinars — mainstream default
Founded 2011. Webinar product separate from Zoom Meeting.

Pricing in 2026: 100 attendees $79/mo; 500 attendees $340/mo; 1000+ custom; 10K Zoom Events $custom.

Features: registration page (basic); reminders; live broadcast; Q&A; polls; chat; recording + replay; HubSpot/Marketo integration; analytics.

Why Zoom: brand recognition; familiar UX; reliable; broad integration ecosystem.

Trade-offs: registration page is plain (most users build a custom one); engagement features less rich than dedicated webinar platforms; pricing climbs at scale.

Pick if: B2B default; familiar UX. Don't pick if: want polished registration page or rich post-event analytics (Demio / Livestorm stronger).

### Demio — modern Zoom Webinars alternative
Founded 2015. Modern UX webinar platform.

Pricing in 2026: Starter $59/mo; Growth $109/mo; Premium $184/mo (50-1000 attendees).

Features: branded registration pages; reminders; engagement (polls, Q&A, handouts); automated replay; HubSpot / Salesforce integrations; analytics.

Why Demio: cleaner UX than Zoom Webinars; better registration pages; fair pricing.

Pick if: SMB-mid-market; want polished UX. Don't pick if: enterprise procurement requires Zoom brand.

### Livestorm — browser-based modern
Founded 2016 (France). Browser-only (no download).

Pricing in 2026: Free (10 attendees); Pro $99/mo; Business $499/mo; Enterprise custom.

Features: browser-based (no download); registration; engagement; replay; integrations.

Why Livestorm: no-download UX (huge for attendance); modern; affordable.

Trade-offs: scaling pricing; less feature-rich than Zoom Webinars at high tier.

Pick if: indie / SMB; want no-download. Don't pick if: large attendee count (pricing).

### ON24 — enterprise demand-gen leader
Founded 1998. Enterprise webinar platform with content hub.

Pricing in 2026: enterprise; typically $30K-200K+/yr.

Features: webinars + content hub + analytics + ABM signals + CRM integration + multilingual + accessibility (closed captions, SCORM); Engagement Hub (cross-event landing).

Why ON24: enterprise-grade; deep analytics for marketing ops; ABM-friendly; high-attendance reliability.

Pick if: enterprise B2B; 50+ webinars/yr; large marketing team. Don't pick if: SMB.

### Goldcast — modern B2B events
Founded 2020. Modern B2B virtual + hybrid events.

Pricing in 2026: $$ tier; typically $20K-100K/yr.

Features: virtual + hybrid events; content hub; engagement; analytics; native B2B mindset.

Why Goldcast: built for B2B (vs. Hopin which leans general); modern UX; growing.

Pick if: B2B mid-market+; want modern alternative to ON24. Don't pick if: SMB scale.

### Bizzabo — hybrid event leader
Founded 2012. Hybrid + virtual events.

Pricing in 2026: enterprise; typically $40K-150K/yr.

Features: hybrid event support (in-person + virtual); event app; check-in; networking; analytics.

Pick if: hybrid events with in-person component. Don't pick if: virtual-only.

### Hopin (now Streamyard / RingCentral Events)
Founded 2019; pivoted multiple times after pandemic peak.

Pricing in 2026: declining; check current product.

Features: virtual events; networking; sessions; expo.

Pick if: virtual events with networking emphasis. Don't pick if: simple webinars.

### WebinarJam / EverWebinar — marketing webinars
Founded 2014. Marketing-funnel-focused.

Pricing in 2026: WebinarJam $499/yr (Basic); EverWebinar $499/yr or bundle.

Features: registration; live + automated; replay; chat; sales-page redirects; integrations with marketing tools.

Why WebinarJam: marketing-funnel-optimized; evergreen.

Pick if: marketing-led webinars; evergreen automation. Don't pick if: enterprise.

### BigMarker — webinar + summit platform
Founded 2010. Webinars + summits.

Pricing in 2026: $79-300+/mo.

Features: webinars; summits (multi-speaker events); branded experience.

Pick if: running summits or virtual conferences at SMB-mid scale.

### Microsoft Teams Webinars / Google Meet Live
Bundled webinar features.

Pricing: bundled with M365 / Workspace.

Features: basic webinars; native to platform.

Pick if: native to your suite; light needs. Don't pick if: marketing-led webinars (need richer features).

### StreamYard / Restream — streaming
Multi-platform streaming.

Pricing: $19-79/mo.

Features: stream to YouTube / LinkedIn / Facebook / Twitter / Twitch simultaneously.

Pick if: public-facing live streams; multi-platform reach. Don't pick if: registered-attendee webinars (use webinar platforms).

## What Webinar Platforms Won't Do

Buying a webinar platform doesn't:

1. **Drive attendance.** You bring the audience. Platform delivers the experience.
2. **Replace good content.** A bad webinar with great UX still loses attendees.
3. **Solve no-shows.** Even with reminders, ~50% of registrants don't attend live. Plan for replay.
4. **Replace CRM workflows.** Lead routing, scoring, nurture happen in CRM. Platform passes data.
5. **Make you a great presenter.** Platform is the room. You're still on stage.

The honest framing: webinars are a top-of-funnel + nurture tool. Platform-level decisions matter; content decisions matter more.

## Webinar Strategy Realities

```text
Honest webinar economics for B2B SaaS in 2026.

Attendance benchmarks:
- Registration → live attendance: 30-50%
- Live attendees → replay views: 20-40%
- Total registrants → replay viewers: 60-80%
- Live attendees → SQL: 5-15% (varies wildly)
- Total cost per SQL: $200-1000 (typical webinar)

Content type performance:
- Customer case studies: highest engagement
- Industry expert / analyst: highest registration
- Product feature deep-dive: lowest engagement, highest qualified leads
- Founder thought leadership: brand-building, slow conversion
- Co-marketed (with partner): often best ROI

Time investments:
- Pre-promotion: 2-4 weeks (LinkedIn + email + ads)
- Day-of: 1.5-2 hour event (45 min content + 15 min Q&A + buffer)
- Post-event: replay distribution, follow-up sequence (7-14 days)
- Total team time: 30-60 hours per webinar

Frequency:
- Monthly cadence common
- Weekly fatigues audience
- Quarterly + on-demand replays scales without burning team

ROI tracking:
- Cost: platform + ad spend + team hours
- Revenue: attribute closed-won deals back to webinars
- 6-month attribution window typical (B2B sales cycle)

Output:
1. Realistic attendance + conversion benchmarks for [INDUSTRY]
2. Content type recommendation for [GOAL]
3. Frequency for [TEAM SIZE]
4. ROI tracking framework
5. When to stop running webinars (signal: no SQLs after 3-month run)
```

The "we'll do weekly webinars" trap: most companies burn out at 4 weeks. Set monthly cadence; layer evergreen on top.

## Pragmatic Stack Patterns

### Pattern 1: First B2B SaaS webinar ($0-99/mo)
- **Livestorm Free** OR **Microsoft Teams** (M365)
- Single webinar; no automation
- Manual follow-up
- Total: $0-99/mo

### Pattern 2: SMB monthly webinars ($79-184/mo)
- **Demio** (Growth or Premium) OR **Zoom Webinars** (100-500 plan)
- HubSpot / Pipedrive integration
- Templated registration + reminders
- Total: ~$100-200/mo

### Pattern 3: Mid-market demand-gen ($300-2K/mo)
- **Demio Premium** OR **Zoom Webinars** + dedicated landing pages
- HubSpot / Marketo integration
- Lead routing + scoring
- Replay distribution

### Pattern 4: Enterprise demand-gen ($30K-200K+/yr)
- **ON24** for content hub + analytics
- 50+ webinars/year
- Marketing ops team owns

### Pattern 5: Virtual conference (1-2x/year, $5-50K)
- **Goldcast** OR **Hopin** OR **Bizzabo**
- 1-3 day event
- Multiple sessions + networking + sponsors

### Pattern 6: Evergreen automation ($499/yr)
- **EverWebinar** for top-of-funnel
- Auto-play with simulated live
- 24/7 lead capture

### Pattern 7: Public live streaming ($25-79/mo)
- **StreamYard** OR **Restream**
- Multi-platform distribution
- Less registration-focused

## Decision Framework: Three Questions

1. **What's the format?**
   - Single webinar → Demio / Zoom Webinars / Livestorm
   - Multi-session conference → Goldcast / Hopin / Bizzabo
   - Evergreen / automated → EverWebinar / WebinarJam
   - Public stream → StreamYard / Restream / YouTube Live

2. **What's your scale + budget?**
   - <100 attendees → Livestorm / Microsoft Teams
   - 100-1000 → Demio / Zoom Webinars
   - 1000+ enterprise → Zoom Enterprise / ON24
   - Conference → Goldcast / Bizzabo

3. **What's your goal?**
   - Lead gen + nurture → ON24 / Demio / Zoom Webinars
   - Brand / thought leadership → StreamYard / Vimeo / YouTube Live
   - Customer training / onboarding → Microsoft Teams / Zoom Webinars / LMS-bundled
   - Virtual conference → Goldcast / Bizzabo / Hopin

## Verdict

For 50% of B2B SaaS in 2026 starting webinars: **Demio** OR **Zoom Webinars**. Mainstream default.

For 20%: **Livestorm**. Browser-based modern.

For 10%: **ON24**. Enterprise demand-gen.

For 10%: **Goldcast** OR **Hopin** OR **Bizzabo**. Virtual events / conferences.

For 5%: **EverWebinar / WebinarJam**. Evergreen automation.

For 5%: **StreamYard / Restream**. Public streaming.

The mistake to avoid: **picking enterprise platform too early**. ON24 is overkill at <30 webinars/year. Use Demio or Zoom until you scale.

The second mistake: **focusing only on platform; ignoring content + audience**. Best platform with weak content / no audience = no leads.

The third mistake: **expecting weekly cadence**. Monthly is sustainable; weekly burns out team. Layer evergreen for steady-state lead gen.
