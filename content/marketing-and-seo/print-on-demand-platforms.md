# Print-on-Demand Platforms: Printful, Printify, Gelato, Spreadshop, Teelaunch, Gooten, Apliiq, Customily

[⬅️ Marketing & SEO Overview](../marketing-and-seo/)

If you're shipping merch, branded products, or a one-off product line in 2026 without holding inventory, you need a print-on-demand (POD) platform. The model: you upload designs; customer orders via your storefront; POD provider prints + ships from their network of facilities; you keep the margin. POD removes inventory + warehousing costs, lets you offer hundreds of SKU variations (apparel sizes, colors, designs) without preordering, and integrates with most e-commerce platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy, eBay).

The tradeoff: per-unit costs are higher than wholesale; customer wait times are longer (3-15 days typical); product quality varies by provider. The right pick depends on product range (apparel-heavy vs. broader SKUs), geographic reach (US-only or global), integration with your storefront, and unit economics.

The first decision is **what products you're selling + where**:

- **Apparel-heavy + US/EU**: Printful or Printify
- **Global / international-first**: Gelato
- **Books / publications**: Lulu, KDP, IngramSpark
- **Marketplace creators**: Society6, Redbubble, TeePublic
- **Premium apparel**: Apliiq
- **Personalized / customized**: Customily, Printful with Customily integration

## Decide What You Need First

POD platforms aren't interchangeable. Different products, scales, and integrations need different providers.

### Indie creator / first store (the 40% case)

You have an audience (Instagram / YouTube / TikTok / Substack) and want merch. Apparel-focused. Don't yet know your demand.

**Pick: Printful** if quality matters; **Printify** if cost matters. Both integrate with Shopify in 5 minutes.

### Premium apparel brand (the 20% case)

Your brand requires premium materials + custom branding (custom labels, hangtags, packaging). Don't want generic POD look.

**Pick: Apliiq** for premium apparel. Some Printful tiers also support label + packaging customization at higher per-unit cost.

### International / EU-first (the 15% case)

Your customers are mostly EU / UK / international. US-focused POD = high shipping costs + slow delivery.

**Pick: Gelato** for global distribution. Local fulfillment in 30+ countries.

### Mid-volume / programmatic (the 10% case)

You're selling hundreds-thousands of orders/month. Need automation + lower per-unit costs through volume tiers.

**Pick: Printful Pro / Printify Premium / Gooten** with API integration + custom workflows.

### Books / publications (the 10% case)

Self-published author, journals, photo books.

**Pick: KDP (Amazon)** for books to Kindle audience; **Lulu Direct** for direct-to-consumer book selling; **IngramSpark** for trade distribution.

### Art prints / specialty (the 5% case)

Photographers, illustrators, artists.

**Pick: Society6 / Redbubble (marketplace)** for low-effort exposure; **Fine Art America** for higher-quality fine-art prints.

## Provider Deep-Dives

### Printful

The dominant POD platform globally. Founded 2013. Latvian-origin, US-headquartered.

Strengths:

- **Broadest product catalog** — apparel, accessories, home decor, books, stationery
- Quality consistent + reliable
- US + EU + Mexico + Canada + Brazil + Australia + Japan fulfillment centers
- **Native integrations** with Shopify, WooCommerce, Wix, Squarespace, Etsy, eBay, Amazon, BigCommerce
- Custom branding options at higher tiers (custom labels, hangtags, packaging)
- Mockup generator for product images
- Fast shipping in US (3-5 days from many regions)
- Strong support
- Sustainability focus (organic cotton, recycled materials options)

Weaknesses:

- **Per-unit prices higher** than Printify and budget POD
- Profit margins thinner unless you mark up significantly
- Some product lines have minimum quality / consistency issues (especially newer additions)

Use Printful when:

- Quality matters
- Apparel + lifestyle products are your focus
- You want a proven, reliable partner

### Printify

Marketplace model — Printify connects you to multiple print providers; you choose which to use per product. Founded 2015.

Strengths:

- **Lowest per-unit prices** — providers compete on pricing
- **Premium plan** ($24.99/mo) unlocks 20% discount on all orders
- Massive provider network — 90+ print providers
- Quality varies BY PROVIDER — pick top-rated providers for consistency
- Integrates with Shopify, Etsy, eBay, WooCommerce, Wix
- Mockup generator
- Wide product range

Weaknesses:

- **Quality + speed depend on which provider you pick** — research per-product
- Customer service is OK; less polished than Printful
- Some less-reliable providers in the network

Use Printify when:

- Cost matters more than max consistency
- You'll vet providers + stick with the reliable ones

### Gelato

Global distributed POD. Founded 2007. Norway-origin.

Strengths:

- **Best global fulfillment** — print centers in 30+ countries
- Local printing reduces customer shipping cost + time dramatically
- Strong sustainability story (local printing reduces shipping carbon)
- Integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy
- Solid product catalog

Weaknesses:

- US-focused product catalog narrower than Printful
- US fulfillment centers fewer than Printful's
- Brand recognition less than Printful in US market

Use Gelato when:

- International audience or EU/Asia-first
- Sustainability is part of your brand
- Local fulfillment matters

### Gooten

API-first POD. Founded 2012. Tech-friendly.

Strengths:

- **Best API + integration story** — programmatic ordering, shipping, tracking
- Good for SaaS / tech-savvy founders building custom flows
- Decent product range + quality

Weaknesses:

- Less brand recognition with consumers
- Smaller catalog than Printful
- Less integrated with major e-commerce platforms (more API + custom)

Use Gooten when:

- You're building custom integration / SaaS that incorporates POD
- You want programmatic ordering at scale

### Apliiq

Premium apparel POD. Founded 2009. US-based.

Strengths:

- **Higher-quality apparel** than mainstream POD
- Custom branding (custom hangtags, neck labels, packaging)
- Premium feel for direct-to-consumer apparel brands

Weaknesses:

- Higher per-unit prices
- Smaller product range (apparel-focused)
- Slower production times

Use Apliiq when:

- Premium apparel brand with brand-quality requirements
- Higher AOV justifies higher unit cost

### Spring (formerly Teespring)

Creator-focused merch. Acquired by Amaze (2024).

Strengths:

- **Built for YouTube creators** — Merch Shelf integration with YouTube
- Free to start; no inventory
- Decent product range
- Solid creator support

Weaknesses:

- Less consistent quality than Printful
- Some pricing opacity

Use Spring when:

- YouTube creator wanting Merch Shelf integration
- Quick start without external storefront

### Society6 / Redbubble (Marketplaces)

Marketplaces where artists upload designs; site handles storefront + ordering + fulfillment + shipping.

Strengths:

- **Low effort** — upload designs; they sell on the marketplace
- Built-in audience
- Wide product range (apparel, home decor, accessories)
- No upfront cost

Weaknesses:

- **Low margins** — typically 10-20% royalty; rest goes to platform
- Limited brand control (you're on their marketplace, not yours)
- Hard to drive traffic; mostly platform-driven

Use Society6 / Redbubble when:

- Artist with designs but no audience / storefront
- Supplemental income source
- Low effort

### Lulu Direct (Books)

Direct-to-consumer book POD. Founded 2002.

Strengths:

- **Best for indie authors** selling direct
- Shopify + Etsy integration
- Quality book printing
- Global distribution

Weaknesses:

- Books + journals only (narrow)
- Per-unit cost higher than offset printing at scale

Use Lulu Direct when:

- Self-published author selling direct (not through Amazon)
- Custom journals / planners

### KDP (Amazon)

Amazon's Kindle Direct Publishing for paperback / hardcover books + ebooks.

Strengths:

- **Massive distribution** through Amazon
- Free to start
- Royalty model: 60% on print books at certain price points
- Decent quality

Weaknesses:

- Locked to Amazon ecosystem
- Margins lower than direct-sale (Lulu) but volume often higher
- Less control over presentation

Use KDP when:

- Books distributed via Amazon
- Self-published author wanting reach

### Bonfire

Nonprofit / fundraiser-friendly tees. Founded 2012.

Strengths:

- **Great for fundraisers** — campaigns with goals + share-with-supporters
- Free to start
- Nonprofit features (501(c)(3) integration)

Weaknesses:

- Apparel-only; narrower than general POD
- Best for time-bound campaigns vs ongoing storefronts

Use Bonfire when:

- Nonprofit fundraiser
- Time-limited campaign / event

### Customily

Personalization layer for POD products.

Strengths:

- **Customer-customizable products** — name on tee; photo on mug
- Integrates with Shopify + Printful + Gelato
- Real-time mockup generation

Weaknesses:

- Adds layer of complexity
- Learning curve for setup

Use Customily when:

- Personalized / custom-name products are core to your brand
- You want customers to design before order

## Profitability Math

POD profit margins are tighter than wholesale. Typical math:

```
retail_price = $30 (T-shirt)
POD cost = $12-18 (printing + product + shipping to customer)
your margin = $12-18

If 50% off on sale: you might be at -$1 margin (loss)
At regular pricing: 40-60% gross margin
```

To make POD profitable:

- **Mark up appropriately** — 2-3x cost minimum
- **Use higher AOV products** — hoodies > tees; 2+ items per order
- **Free shipping above threshold** — increases AOV; you absorb shipping
- **Subscribe + save** for repeat purchases (where relevant)
- **Bundle products** — increases AOV
- **Avoid sales > 30%** unless you understand you're losing per-unit

Don't expect rich margins on individual products. POD is volume + repeat-purchase business.

## Integration Patterns

### Storefront → POD

The default flow:

1. Customer orders on your Shopify / WooCommerce / Etsy store
2. Order auto-routes to POD via integration
3. POD prints + ships
4. Customer receives + tracking notifications
5. POD charges your card; revenue minus cost is your profit

### Custom + API

For programmatic flows:

1. Your app accepts orders (custom storefront, marketplace, etc.)
2. API call to POD with order details + design files
3. Webhook updates on production status
4. Tracking + shipping data flows back

### Multi-POD Routing

Some sellers use multiple PODs based on:

- Product type (apparel from Printful; books from Lulu)
- Geography (Gelato for EU; Printful for US)
- Cost optimization (Printify for budget items; Apliiq for premium)

Tools like **Inkthreadable** and **Gelato Connect** help route per-order.

## What POD Won't Do

**Don't expect POD to compete with wholesale on price.** Wholesale at scale is dramatically cheaper per unit. POD is for low-volume, no-inventory operations.

**Don't expect POD to be fast.** Production typically takes 2-7 days; shipping adds 3-15 days more. Customers wait. Set expectations.

**Don't expect quality identical to wholesale.** Mass-produced consistency is hard with POD. Order samples; check before launching.

**Don't expect POD branding to be invisible.** Your packaging will likely have POD-provider markers unless you pay for premium customization. Customers may notice.

**Don't expect POD to replace your warehouse for high-volume.** Past 1000+ orders/day for a single SKU, switch to wholesale + 3PL.

**Don't expect POD providers to handle returns gracefully.** Returns + exchanges in POD are messy. Have policies.

## Pragmatic Stack Patterns

### Indie creator launching merch

- **Shopify Basic** ($29/mo) for storefront
- **Printful** integration (free; pay per unit)
- 3-5 designs to start
- Customer base from existing audience (YouTube / IG / TikTok)
- Total: $29/mo + per-order costs

### Established DTC brand expanding to merch

- Existing Shopify
- **Printful Pro** for branded packaging
- Consider **Apliiq** for premium apparel
- Total: $0-500/mo + per-order costs

### YouTube creator with merch shelf

- **Spring** integration with YouTube Merch Shelf
- OR Shopify + Printful with custom Merch Shelf integration
- Total: free + per-order costs

### Self-published author

- **KDP** for Amazon distribution
- **Lulu Direct** + Shopify for direct sales
- **IngramSpark** for trade distribution
- Total: per-unit costs + Shopify if using

### International brand

- **Gelato** for global fulfillment
- Shopify integration
- Total: per-unit costs + Shopify

### Multi-product brand at scale

- Multiple POD providers with smart routing
- Tools like Gooten or custom API
- Maybe transition to wholesale + 3PL for top SKUs
- Total: more complex; multiple monthly fees

## Decision Framework: Five Questions

1. **What products?**
   - Apparel: Printful / Printify / Apliiq
   - Books: KDP / Lulu / IngramSpark
   - Art prints: Society6 / Redbubble / Fine Art America
   - Custom products: Customily

2. **Where are customers?**
   - US / NA: Printful / Printify
   - Global / EU: Gelato
   - International niche: Gelato or Gooten

3. **Quality vs. cost priority?**
   - Quality first: Apliiq / Printful Pro
   - Cost first: Printify
   - Balanced: Printful

4. **Storefront platform?**
   - Shopify: Printful / Printify / Gelato all integrate
   - Etsy: Printify / Printful / Gelato integrate
   - Custom / API: Gooten

5. **Scale?**
   - Sub-100 orders/mo: any POD
   - 100-1K orders/mo: Printful + Premium tier or Printify Premium
   - 1K+ orders/mo: consider Gooten API + maybe transition

## Verdict

**Default for most indie sellers**: Printful. Quality + breadth + integrations make it the right pick 70% of the time.

**Cost-conscious / margin-focused**: Printify. Up to 30-40% lower per-unit than Printful for many products.

**Global / EU-first**: Gelato. Local fulfillment dramatically improves customer experience internationally.

**Premium apparel brand**: Apliiq.

**Books**: KDP for Amazon; Lulu for direct.

**Marketplace artists**: Society6 / Redbubble.

**YouTube creators**: Spring (or Printful + Shopify with Merch Shelf integration).

The most common mistakes:

1. **Picking on price alone (Printify) without checking quality.** Cheaper providers have inconsistent quality. Order samples; pick top-rated providers.
2. **Not pricing for POD margins.** Setting prices too low; losing money or breaking even per unit. Mark up 2-3x.
3. **Not setting customer expectations on shipping time.** Customers expect Amazon Prime; POD is slower. Communicate clearly.
4. **Forgetting global shipping cost.** US-only POD shipping to Australia = customer pays $30 shipping. Use regional providers (Gelato) or set expectations.
